RE-empowering rural communities

The Sikat Solar Challenge Foundation, Inc. is a non-stock and   non-profit organization established and incorporated in 2007 committed  to bringing sustainable and impactful change to rural communities  in the Philippines through Renewable Energy (RE) Projects.

Formerly known as the Sikat Solar Car Society, the group began its  journey into RE projects with the development of the first ever  Filipino-made Solar Car, in partnership with De La Salle University  Manila. 3 versions were made of the car and the group raced in the  World Solar Car Race in Australia in 2001, 2011, and 2015.

Addressing energy poverty

Energy poverty has been defined as a general lack of access to  energy-driven services and opportunities that affect productivity,  economic growth, and overall quality of life

As of April 2018*, 2.12 million households in the Philippines are  unelectrified, with 35% of those being poverty-stricken rural  communities in far flung areas.

Source: Department of Energy

Harnessing the talent of the Filipino People

Sikat is committed to to taking a proactive role in addressing  this urgent issue of energy poverty in the Philippines. Serving  as a catalyst in developing innovative and sustainable RE  solutions for far flung and off-grid communities, we execute  programs designed to harness the creativity and talent of the  Filipino youth while promoting the study and application of RE sources.

Sikat is guided by its mission of Inspiring the Youth,  Collaborating with All Stakeholders, and Uplifting the Lives of  Rural Communities and is open to building bridges with advocates  of clean energy.
